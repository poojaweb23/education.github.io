Justices C.K. Prasad and Pinaki Chandra Ghose

July 2, 2014.

The judgment aimed to curb the misuse of arrest powers by the police and emphasized the importance of protecting personal liberty.

The case "Arnesh Kumar vs. State of Bihar" refers to a landmark judgment by the Supreme Court of India that pertains to the arrest and bail provisions under Section 41 and 41A of the Code of Criminal Procedure (CrPC).

In this case, the Supreme Court laid down guidelines to be followed by the police before making an arrest. The judgment highlighted that arrest should not be the default option and that individuals should not be arrested merely based on allegations, especially in non-serious offenses.

Ministry of Law and Justice (India)

The debate surrounding the Uniform Civil Code is ongoing, and its implementation continues to be a topic of discussion in Indian society and politics.

The Uniform Civil Code (UCC) is a proposed set of laws aimed at replacing the diverse personal laws based on religious customs and traditions with a common set of civil laws applicable to all citizens of India regardless of their religion. The idea behind the UCC is to provide a unified legal framework for matters like marriage, divorce, inheritance, and adoption that are currently governed by various personal laws specific to different religious communities.
